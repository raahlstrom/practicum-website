§4.0 — What's the solution?

An SGO keeps students in the pipe.

Too many students fall out at transition points — between elementary and middle school, between middle and high school. A Scholarship Granting Organization is the organizational mechanism that seals those gaps: directing scholarship funds to students exactly when and where continuity breaks down.

§4.4 — What does an SGO require?

The OBBBA outlines 11 requirements for 501(c)(3)s to qualify as SGOs.

To qualify as an SGO and accept contributions eligible for the federal tax credit, an organization must satisfy all eleven requirements. There are no partial credits — miss one and the entire SGO designation is at risk.

Final qualifications may alter slightly once Treasury publishes final rules.
